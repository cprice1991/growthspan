# Growthspan — Brand Guide

The working reference for how Growthspan sounds, looks, and shows up. Keep it
simple. Keep it founder-friendly. Keep the message focused on **speed,
flexibility, and high-impact finance leadership.**

---

## 1. Brand in one line

**Growthspan is an on-demand sprint finance partner for founders.**

Hands-on expertise when founders need it most—spin up in days, pay only for
what you need, wind down cleanly when the work is done.

---

## 2. What Growthspan does

Senior, operator-grade finance and strategy support, delivered in flexible
engagements. Typical work:

- **Fractional / Sprint CFO** — capital strategy, fundraising (debt &
  equity), exits, cash planning.
- **Project CFO engagements** — technoeconomic modeling, project finance
  modeling, annual planning, GTM strategy.
- **Critical BizOps hires** — designing a rigorous process for BizOps,
  Finance, and Chief of Staff roles, plus 1:1 coaching through the first
  year.

> **Principle:** engagements are sized to the problem. A focused 3-week
> sprint is just as valid as a multi-month project.

---

## 3. Who Growthspan is for

**Ideal fit:**

- **Pre-Seed to Series B** startups in **data, AI, software, and climate
  tech**.
- **Technical or first-time CEOs** — scientists, engineers, operators who
  are great at their craft and want a finance partner who can handle both
  modeling and strategy.
- Founders who need **senior help, fast**, but aren’t ready for a full-time
  CFO.
- Teams navigating a **high-stakes moment**: raising, pricing a new
  product, planning the year, making a first business generalist hire,
  modeling a complex transaction.

**Not a fit (be honest about this):**

- Bookkeeping, payroll ops, or controller-level execution.
- Long-term embedded headcount where a FTE is clearly the right move.
- Companies looking for a name on a deck rather than hands-on work.

## 4. Strengths to emphasize

Pull these forward in copy, in pitches, and in every new testimonial intro.

1. **Speed to impact.** Value from day one. Spin up in days.
2. **Flexible scope.** Sprints, projects, or ongoing advisory—easy to start,
   easy to stop.
3. **Both sides of the brain.** Rare combination of rigorous modeling
   **and** strategic/operator judgment. (This is the line multiple
   testimonials reinforce—lean on it.)
4. **Operator, not observer.** Gets in the trenches, asks tough questions,
   runs standups, plays “bad cop” when needed.
5. **Founder-trusted.** Track record with scientist/engineer founders who
   need to translate a business into numbers.

## 6. Voice & tone

Growthspan sounds like a **trusted operator writing to a founder.** Direct,
warm, specific, lightly confident. Never hypey. Never corporate.

### Do
- Write short. Prefer clear statements over padding.
- Name the problem (“first generalist hire,” “complex syndication,”
  “pricing a new SaaS product”) rather than abstract categories.
- Use founder language: **sprint, runway, ICP, standups, data room,
  headcount plan.**
- Use em dashes and simple bullets; let white space do work.
- Bold the single most important phrase in a paragraph—no more.

### Don’t
- Don’t use management-speak: *synergy, leverage (as a verb only), world-class,
  bleeding edge, transform your business.*
- Don’t promise outcomes you can’t own (“we’ll raise your Series A”).
  Describe the support, not guaranteed results.
- Don’t hedge. Avoid *may, might, could, potentially* in headlines.
- No emoji in copy. (Testimonial quotes that include them stay verbatim; UI
  doesn’t add them.)

### Taglines to reuse
- “On-demand sprint CFO for founders.”
- “Hands-on expertise when founders need it most.”
- “Value delivered from day one.”
- “Spin up in days. Only pay for what you need.”

## 7. Visual identity

Kept deliberately simple. The content is the hero; the design is the frame.

### Color palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| Background | Paper | `#f5f0e8` | Page background; warm, un-corporate. |
| Surface | Ivory | `#fffdf8` | Cards, hero card, service cards. |
| Warm surface | Sand | `#ebe4d8` | Section bands (`.section--alt`). |
| Ink | Charcoal | `#1c1917` | Headlines, body emphasis. |
| Muted | Warm gray | `#57534e` | Body copy, secondary text. |
| Accent | Teal | `#0f766e` | Primary action, links, brand accent. |
| Accent hover | Teal bright | `#0d9488` | Hover / focus state. |
| Highlight | Amber | `#b45309` | Eyebrow labels only — sparingly. |
| Hero gradient top | Apricot mist | `#fff7ed` | Top of home hero. |
| Hero gradient mid | Mint mist | `#ecfdf5` | Middle of home hero. |

**Color rules**
- **One accent per screen.** Teal is the brand; don’t introduce new
  accents without updating this guide.
- **Amber is an accent to the accent.** Use on eyebrow labels and rare
  highlights. Never for buttons or long runs of text.
- **No pure black and no pure white** on body surfaces—always the warm
  equivalents above.

### Typography

| Role | Family | Weight |
|------|--------|--------|
| Display / headlines | **Fraunces** | 500–600, tight tracking (`-0.02em`), italic for supporting lines |
| UI / body | **Source Sans 3** | 400 body, 500–600 for emphasis |

**Rules**
- Headlines use Fraunces with a slight negative letter-spacing.
- Italic Fraunces is reserved for the **tagline under the headline** and
  similar supporting lines. Never italicize full paragraphs.
- Keep line length comfortable: body max ~52–60 characters; lede ~42.
- Bold sparingly—only to surface a single important idea.

### Layout & spacing
- Content shell: max width **1080px**; narrow reading shell **680px**.
- Radii: **14px** on cards, **22px** on hero card.
- Generous vertical rhythm using `clamp()`—section padding scales with
  viewport so it never feels cramped or bloated.
- One big idea per section. Aim for the page to feel **quiet.**

### Imagery
- Use real client logos, real headshots, and real screenshots whenever
  possible.
- Avoid stock photography that looks corporate or anonymous.
- If no image is available, use typography and color alone—don’t fill
  space with decoration.

### Logo usage
- Source of truth: `/images/logo.png`.
- Minimum clear space around the mark: height of the icon on all sides.
- Maintain original proportions. Do not recolor, crop, or add effects.
- On dark backgrounds, pair with the paper color behind the logo, or
  commission a proper inverse mark before using it.

## 8. Web & UI patterns

These are the pieces the site uses today; reuse them before inventing new
ones.

- **Buttons:** pill-shaped; primary = solid teal on ivory, ghost = outlined
  teal on ivory. No gradients, no drop shadows.
- **Cards:** ivory surface, 1px warm border, soft long shadow. Used for
  the hero “Built for speed” card, services, and testimonials.
- **Hero card accent:** 4px teal left border on the headline card only.
- **Testimonial block:** teal-tinted left border on the quote,
  attribution with circular initials (or later, a photo) + name + role +
  company.
- **Eyebrow label:** small caps, bold, amber — once per section max.

## 9. Content checklist (before shipping a new page)

- [ ] Page has a **single clear message** (could fit in one sentence).
- [ ] Homepage answers in 5 seconds: *what is this, who is it for, what do
      I do next?*
- [ ] Copy uses real founder language, not abstractions.
- [ ] At most **one bolded idea per paragraph.**
- [ ] One primary CTA, one secondary CTA — not five.
- [ ] Testimonials are verbatim. No paraphrasing.
- [ ] No em-dash-heavy hype paragraphs. (Sparkle over decoration.)
- [ ] Visually quiet: lots of whitespace, no stock photos.
